The Milk Theory




Theory There is a fan theory going around that the Frontman (In-ho) and player 001 (II-nam) might be father and son
Context One

Player 456, Seong Gi-hun, is a divorced, struggling gambler and chauffeur who lives with his elderly mother and deeply cares for his young daughter. Despite his good heart, his life is riddled with debt, failed business ventures, and strained relationships, leaving him desperate for a chance to turn things around. Entering the Squid Game out of sheer necessity, he faces unimaginable challenges that test his morals, humanity, and determination to survive

Oh Il-nam, known as Player 001, is the oldest participant in the Squid Game, joining despite suffering from a brain tumor. Though frail, he displays surprising optimism and cleverness, often serving as a source of wisdom and support for the other players. His presence brings unexpected warmth and complexity to the brutal competition
Reason One

In Season 1, all the players must line up to receive their meal of the day. Gi-hun (Player 456) gets in line ahead of Il-nam (Player 001). When Player 456 reaches the front, he is handed his meal, which includes plain milk. Player 456 informs the guard that he is lactose intolerant and asks for an alternative

As Player 456 walks away, Player 001 mentions that his son was just like him—unable to drink plain milk because he was lactose intolerant too
Context One

In-ho, the Frontman, is a former Squid Game winner who became its ruthless enforcer. Once a player fighting to survive, his alignment with the organization remains unclear, driven by coercion, ambition, or personal stakes. In Season 1, he is revealed as Jun-ho's missing brother, tragically shooting him in a tense confrontation. In Season 2, In-ho enters the games as Player 001, suspicious of Player 456’s return and determined to uncover his plans to end the games

Player 222, Kim Jun-hee, is a pregnant woman who joins the Squid Game, driven by desperation to secure a better future for her unborn child despite the deadly risks involved. Once a struggling street vendor abandoned by her partner, her journey highlights both vulnerability and fierce determination as she fights to survive and protect the life growing inside her

Player 456, Seong Gi-hun, is a down-on-his-luck gambler and a struggling divorced father burdened by debt and a strained relationship with his daughter. In Season 1, he reluctantly enters the Squid Game to win money to turn his life around but emerges as the sole survivor, haunted by the brutality and loss he witnessed. Now determined to expose the organization behind the games, Gi-hun returns with a new resolve, ready to confront the system that exploited him
Reason Two

In Season 2, the Frontman (formerly Player 001) enters the games undercover and becomes allies with Player 456, Player 222, Player 388, and Player 390 after they survive the second game as a team

Later, during mealtime, all the players line up to receive their daily meal. Player 001 receives his portion, which includes a carton of plain milk, and joins his allies—Players 456, 222, 388, and 390—at the table. While they eat, Player 001 notices that Player 222 hesitates over her meal, looking concerned

Player 001 notices that Player 222 seems worried during the meal. Observing her concern, he decides to offer her his carton of milk, sliding it toward her as a gesture of kindness. He suggests that she take it, explaining that her baby would benefit from it more than he would. At first, Player 222 tries to decline, insisting that he should keep it for himself, but Player 001 reassures her. He explains with a gentle smile that he has no problem giving it away since plain milk doesn’t agree with him anymore. Reluctantly, Player 222 accepts his offer, moved by his thoughtful and selfless gesture
Conclusion

Fans have speculated about his deeper connection to the games and the Frontman, In-ho. A popular theory suggests that Il-nam and In-ho might actually be father and son. While the games are full of manipulation and deception, moments like these hint at a hidden personal dynamic, possibly explaining Il-nam's deeper involvement and influence in the games. Could their connection hold the key to understanding the true purpose of the games?
