Player 388 Theory




Theory Theory: Dae-ho (Player 388) is a VIP in the squid games
Context One

Dae-ho is a player in the squid games, his number is Player 388 in the games. He teams up with Player 456, 222, 388, and 390 after the second game. He becomes closer with Player 390 after finding out that they both served time in the military
Reason One

After the third game, Player 456 goes up to Player 456, 120, 388, and 390 about a plan to rebel before the fourth game. The plan consists in taking the guards guns once they grab the players for the fourth game, shoot them, and head to the Frontman’s office to kill him, which would end the games

The plan goes smoothly, but Player 388 is seen having a nervous attack by not shooting or shooting in areas where the guards aren’t even in range while hiding behind a wall. It could be due to the ptsd from his time in the military

While the players are shooting the guards to get to the Frontman’s office Player 120 realizes that they are almost out of ammo. Player 120 suggest she’ll get more from the guards gun’s that they killed through there path. But Player 388 suggests that he could he express’s that he wants to do something useful for the team. Player 120 agrees and Player 388 is off to get the ammo. While he is retrieving the ammo he begins to have a nervous attack again and freezes with all the ammo

Player 120 notices that Player 388 has taken too long to get all the ammo and begins to call him on the radio to communicate. Player 388 never answers and Player 120 goes to see what has happened because their team is very low on ammo now and can no longer reach the Frontman’s office. She finds Player 388 frozen with all the ammo in his hands. She tries to talk to him, but he can only say sorry to her. Player 120 decided to take the ammo from his hands and go back to the team with the ammo, but is stoped by all the guards
Reason Two

For the first games in squid games, red light, green light. All the main characters are introduced playing in the game. Yet, the only player that wasn’t introduced was Player 388. He is introduced after the first game when he goes directly to Player 456 to introduce himself to him. Fans believe he went straight to Player 456 to get close to him and ruin whatever plan he was working on
Reason Three

One of the rules in the squid games is that every game all players can vote x (go home with an equal divided amount of the accumulated money) or o (continue playing the games). Every player was called from Player 456 to Player 001. Once it was Player 388’s turn to vote x or o another man with the number 388 on his shirt appears and is seen going up to the buzzer
Conclusion

Dae-ho’s unusual introduction, suspicious behavior during critical moments, and the presence of a second Player 388 strongly suggest he is more than just a regular contestant. His role as a possible VIP or insider creates a fascinating twist, adding layers of betrayal and control to the Squid Games' narrative. This theory underscores the games' ruthlessness, where even trusted allies may turn out to be enemies working against the players’ survival.
